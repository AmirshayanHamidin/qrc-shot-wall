# The Shot Wall: Measurement Budgets, a Predictive Law, and Design Rules for Quantum Reservoir Computing

*Amirshayan Hamidin — preprint skeleton (v0.4, B5 restoration: pinned-convention generator committed + 8-seed grid re-run, 2026-07-04). Repository: github.com/AmirshayanHamidin/qrc-shot-wall. All numbers below are reproduced from the per-benchmark write-ups in `results/`; each has raw JSON, seeds, and an honesty section. Where an independent audit (`AUDITS.md`) has superseded a write-up's original headline, the restored/audited number is quoted.*

## Abstract

Quantum reservoir computing (QRC) is routinely benchmarked with exact expectation values, but hardware delivers expectations only through finite measurement sampling ("shots"). We present a thirteen-benchmark empirical study of a 4–6-qubit gate-based reservoir under realistic shot budgets. First, the **wall**: with exact readout the reservoir predicts NARMA5 at NMSE 0.003, yet at any practical budget sampling noise erases essentially all added value over a classical linear model on the raw inputs — eight readout-side mitigation strategies all plateau at the no-quantum baseline, and brute force needs ~10⁹ shots per timestep. The lost information is real but unreachable: the reservoir's features are not classically redundant, and the loss occurs at the measurement interface. Second, the wall is **task-shaped**: classification retains ~86% of the quantum benefit at budgets where regression retains ~4%, because coarse outputs hide noise inside decision margins. Third, a **parameter-free law**: noisy classifier accuracy is predicted before any noisy run as the mean probit of margin over projected multinomial shot noise — at R² = 0.939 (0.944 after correcting for observation sampling noise) and MAE 3.3 pp across 150 cells under the restored protocol — committed prediction generator, 8 documented sampling seeds per cell (the originally reported R² = 0.991 / MAE 1.3 pp did not survive an independent audit; see `AUDITS.md`) — and consistent with a physical QPU point (0.886 on ibm_marrakesh, at the predicted shot-noise ceiling). The law extends to gate noise as an effective-shot reduction S·c(γ)² (collapse R² = 0.927), with mapped limits: the residual is shot-irreducible (B7), the scalar factor fails for non-depolarizing channels — coherent errors rotate rather than shrink readouts, and amplitude damping is the genuinely irreducible case (B8) — margins can be estimated from cheap pilot runs after a named bias correction (B9), and the law scores a *retrained* readout, which recovers up to 24.5 pp over the design-time one (B10). Finally, **external validity and design**: the wall, the law's calibration, and the retraining rescue reproduce on an independent Mackey-Glass task family (B11), and a topology sweep identifies information-per-shot (IPS = Σ Δμ²/σ²) as a noiselessly computable design objective that varies 14–56× across coupling graphs (B12) — with the pre-registered caveat that IPS ranks reservoirs only *within* a task, not across tasks (B13). We report five falsified pre-registered hypotheses alongside the confirmations; the negative results carry much of the information.

## 1. Introduction

The promise of quantum reservoir computing is expressivity without training: a fixed quantum dynamical system whose measured observables feed a linear readout. Most reported QRC results, however, evaluate the readout on exact expectation values — a quantity no experiment produces. Every hardware run estimates expectations by sampling, and the estimator's error scales as 1/√S in the shot count S. This paper asks, systematically: **how much of QRC's reported advantage survives the measurement interface, can the survival be predicted in advance, and can it be designed for?**

The study is organized as an arc of thirteen pre-registered benchmarks, each falsifiable, each with raw data and an honesty section in the repository. The arc has four acts: the wall exists (§3), the wall obeys a predictive law (§4), the law has mapped limits (§5), and the law survives an independent task family and yields a design rule with a corrected scope (§6). A derivation sketch of the law is in §7, the ledger of falsified hypotheses in §8.

## 2. Common setup

All benchmarks share one experimental machinery (`src/`): a gate-based reservoir of 4–6 qubits built from random single-qubit rotations and RZZ couplings on a fixed graph; input injection by qubit reset to an input-dependent single-qubit state each timestep (window K = 4–6); 2–4 virtual nodes read by extra unitary applications; features are all single- and two-qubit Pauli-Z expectations (Z₁+Z₂, 27 features per node at 6 qubits); a ridge or logistic readout with a 70/30 chronological split. Exact features come from the density-matrix diagonal; finite-shot features from multinomial sampling of the same distribution — so shot noise is exact, not modeled. Tasks: NARMA regression and parity/majority classification on a binary drive (B1–B10, B12), plus three memory-dependent classification tasks and one-step-ahead prediction on a chaotic Mackey-Glass drive (B11, B13). Classical baselines are tuned echo state networks (hyperparameter grid) and linear/MLP models on the raw input history, feature-count matched. Everything runs on a laptop CPU; the single hardware validation used 6,000 shots on ibm_marrakesh.

## 3. Act I — the wall (B1–B4)

**Fair baselines first (B1).** The reservoir "beats" an untuned classical echo state network by 14×; after an ESN hyperparameter grid, they tie. This calibration step gates everything that follows: all quantum-vs-classical comparisons below use the tuned baseline. (`results/RESULTS.md`)

**The shot wall (B2).** With exact expectations, the 6-qubit reservoir reaches NMSE 0.003 on NARMA5. Under multinomial sampling at realistic budgets, eight readout-side strategies — temporal smoothing, lag stacking, PCA denoising, errors-in-variables ridge with the known noise covariance, sim-trained learned denoisers, shot reallocation across virtual nodes, and combinations — all plateau at the accuracy of a classical linear model on raw inputs with no quantum device. Closing the gap by averaging alone requires ~10⁹ shots per timestep, four to five orders of magnitude beyond practice. Crucially, the buried information is real: neither a linear map nor an MLP reproduces the exact reservoir features from input history, so the advantage exists in the state and dies at measurement. Post-processing cannot help because the loss is upstream of it. (`results/RESULTS_GAP.md`)

**The wall is task-shaped (B3).** On temporal parity — where a linear model on inputs is provably at chance — the same reservoir at the same 40k-shot budget keeps 93% accuracy (86% of its exact-readout benefit), crossing the tuned ESN near 12k shots/step, while NARMA regression at the same budget retains ~4%. Output precision, not the quantum device, sets the wall's height: coarse-output tasks sit below it. (`results/RESULTS_TASKSHAPE.md`)

**Hardware sanity check (B4).** A live run on ibm_marrakesh (6,000 shots, parity-3) delivered 0.886 accuracy — later shown (§4) to be exactly the shot-noise ceiling the law predicts for that configuration.

## 4. Act II — a predictive law (B5–B6)

**The measurement-wall law (B5).** The pre-registered "universal curve in feature SNR × √S" hypothesis *failed* (R² = 0.20); the failure pattern — easy tasks above the curve, hard tasks below — identified the missing variable as the decision margin. The final, parameter-free form: for each test sample, the shot-noise variance along the readout direction w is computable in closed form from the stored measurement distribution, σ² = Σ_nodes [Σ_j p_j q_j² − (p·q)²]/S with q = Zᵀw, and predicted accuracy is the mean of Φ(margin/σ). Across 150 cells (5 tasks × 6 architectures × 5 budgets), the write-up originally reported R² = 0.991 / MAE 1.3 pp — but a full 150-cell audit re-run from the committed code (`AUDITS.md`, 2026-07-04) could not reproduce that precision: the published observations lie outside the seed distribution of the committed pipeline for a subset of cells, the published agreement is tighter than its own 2-seed protocol's noise floor, and the prediction-generator script is missing from `src/`. The headline has since been **restored from committed code**: the prediction generator is now in the repo with its readout convention pinned (`src/qrc_law_predict.py`), and the 150-cell grid was re-run with 8 documented sampling seeds per cell (`src/qrc_law_rerun.py`, per-seed values in `results/law_rerun.json`). The quotable result is **R² = 0.939 (0.944 correcting for observation sampling noise), MAE 3.3 pp, bias −1.0 pp, zero fitted parameters**, improving from R² = 0.86 at 250 shots to 0.96 at 64k; the 8-seed observation noise floor is 0.9 pp, so the residual is law error, not sampling. Still a strong zero-parameter law, at honest precision. This converts B3 from an observation to a mechanism (retention is high where margin × √S ≳ 1) and yields a design tool: simulate noiselessly, read off the hardware budget your task needs before spending quantum time. Downstream benchmarks (B6, B10, B11) quote their own collapse/calibration statistics, which have not yet been re-audited. (`results/RESULTS_LAW.md`)

**Gate noise as effective shots (B6).** A global depolarizing channel of strength γ contracts every decision margin by a noiselessly computable factor c(γ); to leading order gate noise is a pure effective-shot reduction, S_eff = S·c(γ)². Rescaling the shot axis collapses every gate-noisy accuracy curve onto the noiseless one: 420 cells, collapse R² = 0.927 vs 0.851 for ignoring gate noise, advantage growing with noise strength. A practitioner can budget hardware shots accounting for both sampling and fidelity, in advance. Honest residual: the collapse is ~3 pp, not exact. (`results/RESULTS_GATENOISE.md`)

## 5. Act III — the law's limits, mapped (B7–B10)

**B7 (falsified refinement).** The ~3 pp residual was hypothesized to come from per-node depth differences and noise-modified covariance; a pre-registered parameter-free correction was predicted to cut collapse MAE by >30%. It cut it by 2.5% (2.89 → 2.82 pp). Conclusion: gate noise moves the reservoir slightly *off* the accuracy-vs-shots curve — a small shot-budget-irreducible negative bias that no shot-axis rescaling removes. (`results/RESULTS_PERNODE.md`)

**B8 (scope boundary).** Against physically distinct channels the scalar factor degrades: dephasing R² = 0.90, amplitude damping and coherent errors worse; none clears the pre-registered R² > 0.9 bar. The mechanism separates: coherent (unitary) errors *rotate* the readout direction — information-preserving, largely recovered by retraining, wrong sign for a scalar shrinkage — while non-unital amplitude damping (T₁) is the genuinely shot-irreducible case. Under a fixed shot budget, relaxation matters more than calibration error. (`results/RESULTS_BEYONDNOISE.md`)

**B9 (usability at scale).** At unsimulable sizes the law's design-time separation D₀ = ‖μ₁−μ₀‖ must be estimated from a pilot run. The naive plug-in estimator is optimistically biased (shot noise inflates class means) — up to +41% on the hardest task at a 250-shot pilot, exactly where under-budgeting hurts most. A parameter-free correction subtracting the pilot-estimated shot-noise variance removes the bias to ≤0.8%, recovering D₀ to ~3% RMSE at 1,000 pilot shots (≈6% predicted-budget error). (`results/RESULTS_MARGINEST.md`)

**B10 (what the law actually scores).** The closed-form probit is an almost-exact model of the *fixed* design-time readout on a noisy device (R² = 0.948, MAE 0.74 pp) — but that readout is not noise-robust: in the perfect-separation regime it collapses a mean 24.5 pp below what retraining the linear readout on noisy features achieves, and 99.5% of the law-vs-reachable residual is exactly the retraining gain (per-cell correlation −0.9997). The measurement-wall law is really a *retrained*-readout law; on-device readout retraining is load-bearing. (`results/RESULTS_RETRAIN.md`)

## 6. Act IV — external validity and design (B11–B13)

**A second task family (B11).** Swapping the binary drive for chaotic Mackey-Glass with three non-parity memory-dependent classification tasks and chaotic regression, changing nothing else: the wall reproduces and is harsher (the fixed readout collapses to ≈0.56 at 64k shots, *below* the 0.62 classical floor — exact separation on razor-thin margins); the law stays calibrated with zero refit (MAE 2.2 pp, near-zero bias) though the pre-registered R² > 0.9 bar fails at 0.79 because near-chance accuracies compress the variance (reported, not moved); regression shows the same task-shape signature; and retraining again rescues (0.56 → 0.86 at 64k). (`results/RESULTS_TASKFAM.md`)

**Information-per-shot is designable (B12).** Sweeping the coupling graph (chain/ring/star/all-to-all × depth 1–2, 200 cells, matched exact accuracy 1.00) with two design-time quantities — IPS = Σ Δμ²/σ² and the participation ratio PR of the separation: IPS varies 14–56× across topologies and predicts shot-limited accuracy at Spearman ρ = +0.90 at 250 shots (fading to +0.37 by 64k). The star (hub) reservoir wins (0.924 vs 0.79 at 250 shots) *through* higher IPS, not connectivity — all-to-all has the most couplings and the lowest IPS. The concentration hypothesis was falsified: PR is uncorrelated with accuracy (ρ = −0.18), so the design objective is total SNR² per shot, not sparsity. (`results/RESULTS_TOPOLOGY.md`)

**Scope correction at small margins (B13).** Re-running the identical sweep on the Mackey-Glass family: the pre-registered pooled bar fails outright (ρ(log IPS, acc) = +0.07 vs B12's +0.90) because IPS magnitudes are not comparable across tasks with different floors and difficulty. Post-hoc (labeled as such), the effect survives *within* each task (ρ ≈ +0.8 at low budgets, decaying to ~0 at 64k — the wall signature), and "avoid all-to-all" transfers robustly (+4.9 pp, p = 0.002) while the star's edge shrinks to a non-significant +1.9 pp. Corrected design rule: **compute IPS noiselessly for candidate reservoirs on your task and take the argmax; avoid all-to-all coupling and unnecessary depth; do not compare IPS across tasks; do not expect the lever to matter above ~1k shots/step.** (`results/RESULTS_SMALLMARGIN.md`)

## 7. Derivation sketch of the law

Let w be the linear readout trained on exact features, and for test sample t let m_t be its signed decision margin under exact readout. Finite-shot features replace each node's outcome distribution p with the empirical p̂ from S multinomial draws; the feature error Z(p̂−p) is asymptotically Gaussian with the multinomial covariance (diag(p) − ppᵀ)/S. Projected onto the readout, the margin acquires variance σ_t² = Σ_nodes [Σ_j p_j q_j² − (p·q)²]/S, q = Zᵀw. A sample flips classification when the noise exceeds its margin, so P(correct) = Φ(m_t/σ_t), and predicted accuracy is the test-set mean. Every quantity is computable noiselessly at design time; there are no free parameters. Gate noise enters (B6) by contracting margins m_t → c(γ)·m_t while leaving σ_t nearly unchanged, equivalent to S → S·c(γ)² — exact for depolarizing to leading order, approximate beyond (B7), qualitatively wrong for coherent rotations (B8). The statistical ingredients (Gaussian projection of multinomial noise, probit link) are standard; the contribution is the validated application across a wide QRC design grid at ~3.3 pp restored precision, plus the mapped domain of validity. As B10 shows, on-device the probit tracks the fixed readout, and reachable accuracy additionally requires readout retraining on noisy features.

## 8. Honest-negative ledger

Five pre-registered hypotheses were falsified and are reported as failures, not reframed: the naive SNR universal curve (B5 v1, R² = 0.20, failure identified the margin as the true variable); the per-node + covariance refinement of the gate-noise factor (B7, predicted >30% MAE reduction, delivered 2.5%); the scalar fidelity factor beyond depolarizing (B8, no channel clears R² > 0.9); the law's R² > 0.9 bar on the second task family (B11-H2, 0.79 — MAE fine, variance compressed); the concentration/participation-ratio hypothesis (B12-H2, ρ = −0.18); and the pooled IPS→accuracy claim at small margins (B13-H1, +0.07). Two further results are honest partials: B6's collapse is ~3 pp rather than exact, and B13's star advantage is directionally positive but not significant against chain/ring. Separately, a post-publication audit (`AUDITS.md`, 2026-07-04) found the originally reported B5 precision (R² = 0.991 / MAE 1.3 pp) not reproducible from the committed code; this document quotes the restored numbers (R² = 0.939/0.944, MAE 3.3 pp) throughout; the restoration — committed pinned-convention generator plus an 8-seed re-run of the grid — is complete, and the audit's noise-corrected estimate agreed with the independent 8-seed re-run to three decimals (0.944 vs 0.944).

## 9. Limitations

Simulation at 4–6 qubits with exact multinomial sampling and the gate-noise channels of B6–B8; real hardware adds crosstalk, drift, and correlated readout error, so the wall here is *optimistic* — one live-QPU point (0.886, consistent with the law) is a validation, not a hardware study. One injection scheme (reset-based) and one reservoir-parameter seed for the topology sweeps; B13's within-task IPS rescue is post-hoc pending a third-family confirmation; classical comparisons are feature-count matched, not wall-clock matched; the 400-step series with a 70/30 chronological split bounds statistical power (3 sampling seeds per cell; n = 8 per within-task correlation in B12/B13). Task set remains two families. All seeds, raw JSON, and per-benchmark caveats are in `results/`.

## 10. Future work

Confirmatory pre-registered test of the within-task IPS rule on a third task family; reseeded topology sweeps; injection-scheme sweep (the one axis never varied); extending the law to correlated readout error and T₁ channels (B8 identifies amplitude damping as the case that matters); margin-aware readout training (optimize predicted-noisy rather than clean accuracy); and a hardware campaign at fixed shot budget to test the B9 pilot-estimation recipe end-to-end.

## Related work

Our architecture is deliberately standard, in the gate-based lineage of Fujii & Nakajima [Phys. Rev. Applied 8, 024030 (2017)] and Chen, Nurdin & Yamamoto [Phys. Rev. Applied 14, 024065 (2020)], whose IBM-cloud demonstration our B4 echoes; recent scale-ups include 108-atom analog reservoir learning [arXiv:2407.02553] and superconducting analog QRC [Nat. Commun. 15 (2024)]. The measurement problem itself is prior art: Mujal et al. identified measurement back-action and sampling cost as QRC's central obstacle and designed weak/projective protocols around it [npj Quantum Inf. 9, 16 (2023)], and Ahmed, Tennie & Magri quantified finite-sampling degradation in chaotic forecasting and proposed SVD/filtering mitigations [Quantum Mach. Intell. 7, 31 (2025)]. What was not previously located, to our knowledge, is the *floor*: B2 shows eight readout-side strategies — including the SVD-truncation family — plateau at a tuned no-quantum baseline, with the buried features provably non-redundant. Beyond reservoirs, exponential concentration makes kernel values unmeasurable at polynomial shots [Thanasilp et al., arXiv:2208.11060], sampling-noise generalization error has been analyzed for QML broadly [arXiv:2410.14654], and shot-frugal optimizers allocate measurements adaptively [Arrasmith et al., arXiv:2004.06252] — training-side concerns, where our B5 law instead *forecasts* noisy test accuracy in closed form with zero parameters. B6's depolarizing margin contraction is the fixed-dynamics analog of noise-induced barren plateaus [Wang et al., Nat. Commun. 12 (2021)], with B8 mapping where the scalar summary fails (non-unital T₁, coherent rotations). Finally, B2/B10 connect to classical surrogates [Schreiber, Eisert & Meyer, PRL 131, 100803 (2023)]: our exact features admit no surrogate, yet finite shots make the device behave as if they did — the surrogate is created at the measurement interface — and Innocenti et al.'s effective-measurement view of QELMs [Commun. Phys. 6, 118 (2023)] is the natural theoretical frame for reading the law as a per-shot discriminating-power statement. The law's statistical ingredients are textbook; we claim the validated application and its mapped scope, not the mathematics.
