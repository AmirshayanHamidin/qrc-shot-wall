## 1. Quantum reservoir computing: foundations and hardware

Quantum reservoir computing was introduced by Fujii & Nakajima, who showed that the natural dynamics of disordered quantum ensembles can emulate nonlinear dynamical systems with only a trained linear readout [Phys. Rev. Applied 8, 024030 (2017); arXiv:1602.08159]. Chen, Nurdin & Yamamoto established a universality class for gate-based quantum reservoirs and gave the first cloud-QPU demonstration on IBM hardware [Phys. Rev. Applied 14, 024065 (2020); arXiv:2001.09498]; Suzuki et al. later used the device's own dissipation as the reservoir ("natural QRC") [Sci. Rep. 12 (2022)]. At larger scale, Kornjača et al. ran gradient-free reservoir learning on up to 108 neutral atoms on QuEra's Aquila [arXiv:2407.02553], and an analog superconducting oscillator-qubit reservoir was demonstrated for microwave classification [Nat. Commun. 15 (2024); arXiv:2312.16166]. Domingo et al. even report regimes where moderate hardware noise *helps* [Sci. Rep. 13 (2023)].

**Position.** Our gate-based, reset-injection, Pauli-Z-feature reservoir (§2 of the preprint) is squarely in the Fujii-Nakajima/Chen-Nurdin lineage; nothing about the architecture is novel, by design. What the hardware papers generally do not do is compare against *tuned* classical baselines at matched feature counts under an explicit shot budget — B1's result (a 14× "advantage" over an untuned ESN collapses to a tie after a hyperparameter grid) says this fairness step is load-bearing for every claim downstream.

## 2. The measurement problem in QRC (closest prior art)

The direct predecessor of this program is Mujal, Martínez-Peña, Giorgi, Soriano & Zambrini, "Time-series quantum reservoir computing with weak and projective measurements" [npj Quantum Inf. 9, 16 (2023); arXiv:2205.06809], which identified measurement as QRC's central practical obstacle: projective readout destroys the reservoir memory that sequential processing needs, and estimating expectations costs shots. They design protocols (restart-and-rewind, weak measurement) that trade back-action against sampling noise and show ideal performance is *approachable* with finite resources on memory/forecasting tasks. Ahmed, Tennie & Magri, "Optimal training of finitely sampled quantum reservoir computers" [Quantum Mach. Intell. 7, 31 (2025); arXiv:2409.01394] is the closest recent work: they quantify how sampling noise degrades chaotic-forecasting QRC (worse for recurrent than recurrence-free variants, since noise recirculates) and propose readout-side mitigations — SVD truncation of the noisy feature matrix and frequency-domain filtering. Feedback-based minimalistic QRC [npj Quantum Inf. 11 (2025); arXiv:2412.17817] likewise treats finite sampling as a first-class constraint.

**Position — what was already known vs. what this repo adds.** That shot noise degrades QRC, and that it can dominate the error budget, is established (both papers above). B2's contribution is the *shape and location of the floor*: eight readout-side strategies — including exactly the PCA/SVD-truncation family Ahmed et al. advocate, plus errors-in-variables regression with the *known* noise covariance and sim-trained denoisers — all plateau at the accuracy of a classical linear model on raw inputs, i.e. at the no-quantum-device baseline, with a ~10⁹ shots/step brute-force requirement to actually close the gap. We use restart-based (rewind) readout throughout, so our wall is about sampling statistics, not back-action; weak-measurement protocols à la Mujal et al. attack a different term of the same budget and are complementary. B2's redundancy check (neither a linear map nor an MLP reproduces the exact features from input history) rules out the deflationary reading under which the plateau would be unsurprising because the features were classically available anyway.

## 3. Shot noise across quantum machine learning

Outside reservoirs, measurement cost is a recognized structural obstacle. Thanasilp, Wang, Cerezo & Holmes showed quantum kernel values can concentrate exponentially, so polynomial shot budgets yield trivial models [arXiv:2208.11060; Nat. Commun. (2024)] — the same "information present but unmeasurable" mechanism as our wall, in kernel form. Sampling-noise generalization error has been analyzed for QML models more broadly [arXiv:2410.14654], and variance-regularized training reduces finite-sampling error in quantum neural networks [Quantum 8, 1385 (2024)]. On the resource-allocation side, shot-frugal optimizers such as Rosalin adaptively distribute measurements across operators [Arrasmith, Cincio, Somma & Coles, arXiv:2004.06252].

**Position.** These lines quantify or mitigate shot noise for *training* variational models. The B5 law is different in kind: a zero-parameter, closed-form *forecast* of noisy test accuracy — mean Φ(margin/σ) with σ the multinomial covariance projected on the readout — computable entirely at design time, validated at ~1 pp over 150 cells and on a physical QPU. The statistical ingredients (Gaussian limit of multinomial sampling, probit link) are textbook; we claim novelty only for the validated application to QRC readouts and the mapped domain of validity (B7–B11), not for the mathematics. B12's IPS objective (Σ Δμ²/σ² per shot) is likewise a standard discriminant SNR repurposed as a *design* criterion for reservoir topology; its within-task validity and cross-task failure (B13) is, to our knowledge, the first such scoping in QRC, but SNR-per-resource criteria have long histories elsewhere and we do not claim conceptual priority.

## 4. Gate noise as an effective model

Wang et al. proved depolarizing-type noise flattens variational cost landscapes exponentially (noise-induced barren plateaus) [Nat. Commun. 12 (2021), doi:10.1038/s41467-021-27045-6] — the same mechanism (Pauli/depolarizing channels contracting operator components toward the identity) that underlies B6's margin-contraction factor c(γ) and its effective-shot reading S_eff = S·c(γ)². B6-B8 are the fixed-dynamics analog: no trainability question, but a quantitative account of *which* channels admit a scalar summary (depolarizing yes, dephasing marginal, coherent rotations no — wrong sign, retrainable; amplitude damping no — genuinely irreducible). The non-unital exception matches the broader finding that non-unital noise behaves qualitatively differently from unital noise in variational settings [see e.g. Quantum 9, 1617 (2025) on non-unital NIBPs].

## 5. Classical surrogates and what "quantum advantage" survives

Schreiber, Eisert & Meyer showed large classes of quantum learning models admit efficient classical surrogates that reproduce their input-output behavior [Phys. Rev. Lett. 131, 100803 (2023); arXiv:2206.11740]. Our B2 redundancy test is the reservoir-side cousin of the surrogate question, with the opposite outcome and a twist: the exact reservoir features are *not* classically reproducible from input history in our setting — but at realistic shot budgets the measured system performs *as if* they were. The practical surrogate is created by the measurement interface, not by the model class. Innocenti et al.'s effective-measurement framework for quantum extreme learning machines [Commun. Phys. 6, 118 (2023); arXiv:2210.00780] gives a clean theoretical language for this: the reservoir + finite-shot readout *is* a single noisy effective measurement, and everything in the B5 law can be read as characterizing that measurement's discriminating power per shot.
