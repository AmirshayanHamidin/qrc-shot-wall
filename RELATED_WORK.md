## 1. Reservoir computing and QRC foundations

Reservoir computing originates classically with echo state networks (Jaeger, GMD Tech. Rep. 148, 2001; Jaeger & Haas, *Science* 304, 78, 2004) and liquid state machines (Maass, Natschläger & Markram, *Neural Comput.* 14, 2531, 2002); the delay-based single-node implementation of Appeltant et al. (*Nat. Commun.* 2, 468, 2011) introduced the time-multiplexed "virtual node" idea that our reservoir reuses in quantum form. Quantum reservoir computing was proposed by Fujii & Nakajima (*Phys. Rev. Applied* 8, 024030, 2017; arXiv:1602.08159), with spatial multiplexing follow-ups (Nakajima et al., arXiv:1803.04574) and an NMR spin-ensemble experiment (Negoro et al., arXiv:1806.10910). The field is surveyed by Mujal et al. (*Adv. Quantum Technol.* 4, 2100027, 2021; arXiv:2102.11831). Our common setup (§2 of the preprint) is a standard gate-based instance of this family; nothing in the architecture is novel, deliberately — the program's claims are about the *measurement interface* of a representative reservoir, not about a new reservoir.

**B1 (fair baselines).** The observation that untuned classical baselines flatter quantum models is not new in spirit — Huang et al.'s power-of-data analysis (*Nat. Commun.* 12, 2631, 2021) and the classical-surrogate line (Schreiber, Eisert & Meyer, *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 131, 100803, 2023; arXiv:2206.11740) both show that claimed quantum learning advantages often evaporate against properly equipped classical models. Recent QRC-specific benchmarking (Kodali et al., arXiv:2510.25183, 2025 — QRC vs tuned ESN/LSTM on NARMA-10) reaches the same "competitive, not dominant" conclusion our B1 does. B2's redundancy test (neither a linear map nor an MLP reproduces the exact reservoir features from input history) is a small, task-specific instance of the surrogate question: the features are *not* dequantizable in that narrow sense, which is precisely what makes their loss at the measurement interface a real loss.

## 2. Finite sampling as the central bottleneck (B2)

The closest prior thread to the shot wall:

- **Mujal et al.** (*npj Quantum Inf.* 9, 16, 2023; arXiv:2205.06809) is the canonical treatment of measurement in time-series QRC: projective measurement destroys the reservoir memory needed for online operation, and they design protocols (restart/rewind, weak measurement) that trade measurement backaction against memory. Our setting deliberately sidesteps backaction (restart-style evaluation from stored distributions) to isolate the *statistical* cost of sampling — the wall we quantify is what remains after their problem is solved.
- **Hu et al.** (*Phys. Rev. X* 13, 041020, 2023; arXiv:2307.16083) is the closest conceptual neighbor of the whole program. Their "resolvable expressive capacity" (REC) bounds what any finitely-sampled physical system can express, and their "eigentasks" are the functions extractable at minimal shot noise, demonstrated on superconducting hardware. Two differences in emphasis: (i) REC is a task-agnostic capacity of the system, whereas our measurement-wall law (B5) is a *task-conditional, per-cell accuracy prediction* (margin over projected multinomial noise, zero fitted parameters, R² = 0.991 over 150 cells); (ii) our IPS design objective (B12) — total class-discrimination SNR² per shot — is likely expressible in their framework as a task-projected capacity, a correspondence we have not yet formalized (flagged in Honesty section below).
- **Exponential concentration** (Thanasilp et al., arXiv:2208.11060; *Nat. Commun.*, 2024) proves that quantum-kernel values can concentrate exponentially in qubit count, making polynomial shot budgets insufficient in principle. Our wall is the same phenomenon's *finite-size, empirical* face: at 4–6 qubits nothing is exponential yet, and the interesting structure (task shape, margins, topology dependence) lives exactly in the pre-asymptotic regime that concentration theorems do not resolve.
- **Mitigation under finite sampling.** "Optimal training of finitely-sampled quantum reservoir computers" (arXiv:2409.01394; *Quantum Mach. Intell.*, 2025) shows SVD- and filter-based denoising of noisy reservoir activations improves chaotic forecasting, and that recurrence-free architectures degrade less because sampling noise does not propagate through the loop. "Reorganizing Quantum Measurement Records Improves Time-Series Prediction" (arXiv:2604.28160, 2026) improves prediction by restructuring how finite measurement records are converted to features at a fixed shot budget. Both are readout-side strategies of exactly the class our B2 grid tested eight variants of; our negative result (all plateau at the no-quantum classical baseline on NARMA) does not contradict their gains — their baselines are the *noisy* quantum model, ours is the *classical* model the noisy quantum model must beat to justify the hardware. Read together: denoising moves you up the noisy curve, but on small-margin regression tasks the curve tops out at the classical floor.

**B3/B11 (task shape).** The observation that classification tolerates output noise better than regression is implicit in Hu et al.'s classification demonstrations and standard quantization arguments, but we have not found a prior *quantitative retention comparison* at matched reservoir/budget (86% vs 4% at 40k shots), nor the B11 result that razor-thin exact margins can push a quantum readout *below* the classical floor at realistic budgets.

## 3. Predicting accuracy before the run (B5, B9)

The law's statistical ingredients — Gaussian projection of multinomial sampling noise onto a linear readout, probit link from margin/σ to flip probability — are textbook. Its nearest relatives are the REC bound of Hu et al. (capacity, not per-task accuracy) and, further afield, margin-based generalization arguments in classical learning theory. We found no prior work that predicts *noisy QRC accuracy per task/architecture/budget cell, in closed form, from noiseless quantities, with zero fitted parameters, at ~1 pp calibration* — nor prior work on the pilot-estimation bias of B9 (shot-noise inflation of estimated class separations and its parameter-free correction), though the bias itself is the classical "naive Euclidean distance between noisy means" problem known in statistics; the contribution is noticing it silently corrupts quantum shot budgeting at scale.

## 4. Gate noise: obstacle, resource, and effective model (B6–B8)

Three strands of prior work frame B6–B8:

- **Noise as contraction.** Noise-induced barren plateaus (Wang et al., *Nat. Commun.* 12, 6961, 2021; arXiv:2007.14384) show local Pauli/depolarizing noise exponentially flattens VQA landscapes with depth, and Stilck França & García-Patrón (*Nat. Phys.* 17, 2021; arXiv:2009.05532) bound what noisy circuits can achieve before becoming classically matchable. Our B6 margin-contraction factor c(γ) is the fixed-readout, reservoir-computing analogue of the same mechanism: depolarizing noise shrinks signal toward the maximally mixed point, and because QRC trains nothing variationally, the effect surfaces not as vanishing gradients but as a computable effective-shot reduction S·c(γ)². B7's shot-irreducible residual and B8's channel taxonomy (coherent errors rotate readouts — recoverable; amplitude damping is the genuinely irreducible case) map where that leading-order picture ends, consistent with the general lesson that non-unital channels behave qualitatively differently from unital ones.
- **Noise as resource.** Quantum-noise-induced reservoir computing (Kubota et al., arXiv:2207.07924) and natural QRC on IBM hardware (Suzuki et al., arXiv:2107.05808; *Sci. Rep.*, 2022) use device dissipation as the fading-memory mechanism itself. This is complementary, not contradictory: those works exploit noise in the *dynamics*, while our wall concerns noise in the *readout statistics*; a noise-driven reservoir still pays the B2 sampling cost at its measurement interface. Related resilience results (e.g. "Quantumness can enhance resilience to statistical noise in spin-network QRC", *Front. Phys.*, 2026; "Role of coherence in many-body quantum reservoir computing", *Commun. Phys.*, 2024) study when quantum correlations mitigate statistical noise — our IPS objective gives one concrete mechanism such resilience must operate through (raising Δμ²/σ² per shot).
- **Retraining on noisy features (B10).** The trainable temporal postprocessor of Khan et al. (*PRX Quantum* 5, 020364, 2024) learns readouts directly on measured (hence noisy) records, and hardware QRC works train on device data as a matter of course. B10's contribution is quantifying what that choice is worth (up to 24.5 pp over the design-time readout; 99.5% of the law-vs-reachable residual) and the resulting reinterpretation of the law as a *retrained*-readout law.

## 5. Reservoir design under a measurement budget (B12–B13)

Design criteria for quantum reservoirs are usually dynamical: the thermal/ergodic phase transition (Martínez-Peña et al., *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 127, 100502, 2021; arXiv:2103.05348), Krylov complexity and observability (arXiv:2502.12157 and the *Phys. Rev. Research* follow-up "Engineering quantum reservoirs through Krylov complexity, expressivity, and observability"), entanglement structure in spin networks (arXiv:2403.08998; arXiv:2511.04900), or general quantum advantage conditions ("Exploring quantum mechanical advantage for reservoir computing", arXiv:2302.03595). These optimize what the reservoir *represents*. B12's point is orthogonal: at fixed (perfect) exact-readout accuracy, topologies differ 14–56× in *information per shot*, so the binding design constraint at realistic budgets is statistical, not representational — and the winning knob (total SNR² per shot; hub topology; avoid all-to-all and unnecessary depth) is computable noiselessly at design time. The closest 2026 development we found, "Kernel-based optimization of measurement operators for quantum reservoir computers" (arXiv:2602.14677), optimizes the *measurement* side directly, which is naturally read as optimizing an IPS-like objective over observables rather than over couplings; a comparison is future work. B13's scope correction (IPS ranks reservoirs within a task only, and the lever dies above ~1k shots/step) has no analogue we found — cross-task comparability of such design metrics seems generally unexamined.

## 6. What this program adds, in one paragraph

Prior work established that measurement is *a* problem for QRC (backaction: Mujal et al. 2023; capacity: Hu et al. 2023; asymptotics: Thanasilp et al.) and proposed mitigation on the dynamics, protocol, or postprocessing side. This program contributes, empirically and at honest small scale: (i) a quantified end-to-end wall on a standard benchmark with eight mitigation strategies jointly nulled against a *tuned classical* baseline (B1–B2); (ii) the task-shape decomposition of the wall (B3, B11); (iii) a zero-parameter, ~1 pp-calibrated accuracy law usable before any noisy run, with its domain of validity mapped rather than asserted — gate-noise extension, non-depolarizing failure modes, pilot-estimation recipe, and the retrained-readout reinterpretation (B5–B10); and (iv) information-per-shot as a validated, noiselessly computable *design* objective with an honest scope correction (B12–B13). Five falsified pre-registered hypotheses are reported as such.

## Honesty section — method and limits of this literature pass

- **Method.** Web search (7 thematic queries) plus abstract-level reading on 2026-07-03, from a fresh session with no library access. Venue/volume details were verified by search or abstract-page fetch for the load-bearing citations (Fujii & Nakajima 2017; Mujal et al. 2021, 2023; Hu et al. 2023; Thanasilp et al.; Wang et al. 2021; Stilck França & García-Patrón 2021; Schreiber et al. 2023; Martínez-Peña et al. 2021; Khan et al. 2024; arXiv:2409.01394; arXiv:2510.25183).
- **Not full-text reads.** Positioning statements rest on abstracts and, for a few items, search-result summaries. Two 2026 items (arXiv:2604.28160; arXiv:2602.14677) are cited from title + indexed summary because the abstract fetch returned unreadable content; their one-line characterizations here should be re-checked against the papers before arXiv submission. Classical RC citations (Jaeger; Maass; Appeltant) and Negoro et al. are from standard knowledge, not re-fetched.
- **Known open risk.** The relationship between our IPS (B12) and Hu et al.'s resolvable expressive capacity / eigentask framework is asserted as "likely expressible within" but has **not** been worked out; if IPS turns out to be exactly a known REC quantity, B12's novelty claim narrows from "new objective" to "first topology sweep of a known objective". This is the single highest-priority full-text read before submission.
- **No exhaustiveness claim.** Photonic/analogue QRC platform literature, error-mitigation theory, and shadow-tomography-based readout proposals were deliberately left shallow; none appeared to preempt the specific claims above, but the search was not designed to prove absence.
